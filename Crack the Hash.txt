Key Takeaways

-Weak passwords remain the primary vulnerability regardless of hashing algorithm.

-Unsalted hashes (MD5, SHA1) are highly vulnerable to rainbow table attacks.

-Modern hashing algorithms like bcrypt and SHA512crypt provide stronger protection when combined with strong passwords.

Hash cracking knowledge is relevant for:

Credential dumping investigations
Password security audits
Incident response
Security Implications

Organizations must:
Avoid MD5 and SHA1
Enforce strong password policies
Implement salted adaptive hashing (bcrypt/Argon2)
Monitor credential leaks and dumps
